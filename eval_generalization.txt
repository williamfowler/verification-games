MOTIVATION
----------
The shipped PASS (held-out max err 8.40%, seed 12345) is a *random-split* result:
train and test are interpolation draws from one workload family. These analyses
ask two harder questions using the same measured records, no new sweeps:
  1. Family holdouts — does the fit extrapolate to a config family it never saw?
  2. Stability — how much do the fitted constants and the PASS/FAIL verdict
     depend on which random split you happened to draw?

  Verdict (2-param): all five families stay under 10%, i.e. the frontier-regime

  five land within 0.5 pt of the line, and d_model<=256 is systematically ~9%

  Verdict (3-param/EMC): FAILS the d_model>=640 holdout at 21.7%. When the
  high-d_model (high arithmetic-intensity) runs are unseen, the blind-fitted
  E_PER_TB_J extrapolates badly — direct evidence the byte coefficient is not
  determined by this pool (see conditioning diagnostic in old/eval_results.txt:
  corr(gt,tb)=0.912, cond=1.3e8).

  Findings:

    the 2-param model (60% with the 3-param). The shipped seed-12345 PASS at
    8.40% sits near the middle of a 3.6%-12.6% distribution.
  * The 2-param constants are only loosely pinned: E_MARGINAL spans 5.37-7.72
    J/TFLOP (+/-19% around the shipped 6.23) and P_OVERHEAD spans 2.50-4.00 W.

    tail — on small train subsets the LOO objective can still chase the bound.
  * E_PER_TB_J is effectively unidentified on benign data: 1297-8581 J/TB
    (6.6x spread; sd ~38% of mean). Its shipped value (3857.9) is the center of
    a very wide distribution, consistent with the promotion note ("weakly
    determined") — and with the d_model>=640 holdout failure above.

INTERPRETATION
--------------
* The 2-param frontier estimator is more robust than the skeptical prior
  suggested — family extrapolation holds — but its 10% margin is thin, and the
  single-split PASS overstates confidence (~1 in 4 resampled splits fail).
  A fairer headline for the MVP is "typically ~9% held-out max error on
  frontier transformer runs, with occasional splits up to ~12.6%".
* The overhead/marginal decomposition (and therefore any physical reading of
  the constants) is NOT trustworthy: the two parameters trade off across
  splits. Only their combined prediction on frontier-like runs is stable.
* The EMC/3-param model, on benign data alone, is strictly less stable than
  the 2-param model. Its value must be adversarial (catching off-line
  workloads, see adversarial probe) — it should not be sold as an accuracy
  improvement, and its constants should be treated as provisional until fit
  on data with real arithmetic-intensity spread.

UPDATE 2026-07-07 — v2 sweep (eval_results_v2.txt): precision + architecture axes
==================================================================================
The 38-config v2 sweep added nhead {2,8}, dim_feedforward {3072,4096}, seq 512,
SGD, and a PRECISION axis (fp32 baseline / tf32 / fp16 / bf16), fit
precision-BLIND (the monitor cannot observe precision). Same 642.0 mW baseline.

PRECISION BREAKS THE ESTIMATOR (the headline)
---------------------------------------------
* The blind fit over all 23 frontier runs FAILS: held-out max err 16.37%; the
  two tf32 outliers also degenerate the fit itself (P_OVERHEAD driven to the
  0 W boundary, E_MARGINAL 6.2 -> 10.9), dragging even fp32 runs to 14.8%.
* tf32 runs (tensor cores ON): ~2x more FLOPs/joule than fp32 -> read ~50% LOW.
* fp16/bf16 runs at this model scale drop to ~60% GPU util — BELOW the frontier
  gate — while doing up to 146 true TFLOPs, and read ~70% LOW vs fp32 constants.
  Precision is therefore a working red-team evasion channel on BOTH axes: it
  defeats the util gate and the energy conversion (underestimates of 2-3.5x, in
  the verifier-dangerous direction). This is physics, not mis-calibration: a
  joule meter cannot see precision. Estimates must be read as a LOWER BOUND
  w.r.t. precision.

FP32-ONLY SUBSET (21 frontier runs, incl. all new architecture axes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
* Random split (seed 12345): PASS, held-out max 8.95%; ship fit max 8.82%.
* The new architecture axes are benign: holdout errors — optimizer=sgd 1.93%,
  seq_len>=512 4.32%, num_layers=6 3.46%; marginal fails at
  dim_feedforward>=3072 (10.06%) and the persistent d_model<=256 bias (10.78%).
* Stability over 200 splits IMPROVED vs v1: E_MARGINAL 5.29-6.81 (sd 0.37 vs
  0.62), split-failure rate 13.5% (vs 23%). Architecture diversity helped
  identify the fit. 3-param remains unstable (59% of splits fail).
* Constants updated 2026-07-07 to the v2 fp32 ship fit (5.72 J/TFLOP, 3.75 W;
  EMC 5.61 / 4621.7 / 1.463): the old constants let one v2 run breach 10%
  (10.44%), the new fit holds all 21 at <=8.82%.

ACTMON BYTE SCALE (power_calibration/actmon_scale_bench.py, 2026-07-07)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
* Measured scale factor k = 0.0133 actmon-TB per true TB (counter reads ~1.3%
  of true traffic; linear, idle background negligible).
* The fitted E_PER_TB_J converts to 61.5 J per TRUE TB — inside the LPDDR5
  physical ballpark (~50-150 J/TB). The blind byte coefficient is physically
  sensible, with the caveat that a pure-streaming spoof's TOTAL energy is
  ~40 J/TB, slightly below it — which is why estimate_tflops_emc over-corrects
  spoofs to large negative values (fine for a tripwire, wrong as a meter).

UPDATE 2026-07-08 — 15W power-mode replication (pseudo-other-hardware test)
==================================================================================
The full calibration methodology was re-run from scratch in nvpmodel mode 0
(15W) as if on new hardware: fresh idle baseline (642.6 mW), fp32 pool
(26 configs, 22 cleared the frontier gate), fresh fit, fresh verdict
(eval_results_15w.txt / eval_results_15w_records.json).

DOES THE METHODOLOGY REPLICATE?  YES (with the same soft-PASS caveat)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
* Fresh 15W fit: held-out max err 9.20% PASS; all 22 frontier runs <= 8.92%.
* Fresh constants differ hugely from 25W — E_MARGINAL 3.92 vs 5.72 J/TFLOP
  (the 15W operating point is ~30% more FLOP-efficient; DVFS), P_OVERHEAD 2.05
  vs 3.75 W. Same methodology, completely different numbers: the procedure is
  what transfers, not the constants.

DO THE 25W CONSTANTS TRANSFER?  NO — CATASTROPHICALLY
------------------------------------------------------
* 25W constants scored on the 22 15W frontier runs: signed error mean -95%
  (range -118% to -87%; some estimates go negative). An unrecalibrated monitor
  on a different power mode is blind, not merely biased. This is the concrete
  justification for the CALIBRATION_FINGERPRINT startup guard, which flags
  exactly this condition (the 15W records' fingerprint reads nvpmodel_mode=15W).

* At 25W the byte term was a collinear passenger (59% split-failure); at 15W it

  vs 12.5%, and a tighter E_PER_TB distribution). Interpretation: in the
  compressed 15W power band, per-workload arithmetic-intensity differences are
  a larger fraction of the energy signal, so the byte term explains real
  variance. The 2-param single-split PASS (9.20%) sits on the lucky side of a
  12.5%-mean distribution — at 15W the EMC estimator, not the 2-param one,
  would be the meter of choice. Role assignments are power-mode-specific.
